# Process Map — Example

> **This is a public-safe illustrative example.** All organization names, processes, and context are fictional. This demonstrates how an organization might document a process for agent consumption.

---

## [DemoOrg] — Client Onboarding Process

*Version 1.2 | Owned by: [Client Operations Lead] | Reviewed: Quarterly*

---

### Purpose

This document describes the standard client onboarding process at [DemoOrg]. It is the authoritative reference for any agent assisting with onboarding tasks.

---

### Process Overview

```
Client contract signed
        │
        ▼
Step 1: Internal setup (Day 1–2)
        │
        ▼
Step 2: Welcome and kickoff scheduling (Day 2–3)
        │
        ▼
Step 3: Kickoff meeting (Day 3–5)
        │
        ▼
Step 4: Needs assessment (Week 2)
        │
        ▼
Step 5: Engagement plan confirmed (Week 3)
        │
        ▼
Onboarding complete → Handoff to delivery team
```

---

### Step Details

#### Step 1: Internal Setup (Day 1–2)

**Owner:** Client Operations

**Actions:**
- Create client record in CRM
- Set up client folder in SharePoint
- Assign engagement lead and support team
- Brief engagement lead on client background

**Agent use:** An agent may assist with drafting the client brief but must not create CRM records autonomously.

---

#### Step 2: Welcome and Kickoff Scheduling (Day 2–3)

**Owner:** Engagement Lead

**Actions:**
- Send welcome email to primary client contact
- Propose 3 kickoff meeting times within the next 5 business days
- Confirm meeting and send calendar invite with agenda

**Agent use:** An agent may draft the welcome email and propose meeting times. All communications must be reviewed and sent by the Engagement Lead.

---

#### Step 3: Kickoff Meeting (Day 3–5)

**Owner:** Engagement Lead

**Actions:**
- Conduct kickoff meeting (agenda: introductions, scope review, communication preferences, next steps)
- Document meeting notes in client SharePoint folder
- Send follow-up summary to client within 24 hours

**Agent use:** An agent may draft meeting notes and the follow-up summary from transcription input. Engagement Lead reviews before sending.

---

#### Step 4: Needs Assessment (Week 2)

**Owner:** Engagement Lead + Specialist

**Actions:**
- Conduct structured needs assessment session
- Document findings in standard needs assessment template
- Identify any scope additions or constraints

**Agent use:** An agent may assist with structuring the assessment document. No autonomous client communication at this stage.

---

#### Step 5: Engagement Plan Confirmed (Week 3)

**Owner:** Engagement Lead

**Actions:**
- Draft engagement plan based on needs assessment
- Internal review by [Delivery Director]
- Present and confirm plan with client
- Update CRM with confirmed engagement details

**Agent use:** An agent may draft the engagement plan. Human review is required before client presentation.

---

### Escalation Triggers

An agent must escalate to the Engagement Lead when:

- A client request falls outside the defined scope of the engagement
- There is ambiguity about the correct next step
- A client expresses dissatisfaction at any point in the process

---

### Out-of-Scope Actions

No agent involved in this process should:

- Modify contract terms or commitments
- Approve scope changes
- Communicate directly with clients without Engagement Lead review
